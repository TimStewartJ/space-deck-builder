📖 Glossary — click to expand
Early / Mid / Late (Game Phases)
Turns 1–5 = early, 6–15 = mid, 16+ = late. Used to track how the agent's strategy evolves over the course of a game.
Policy Entropy
Measures how uncertain the agent is about its next action. High entropy = many viable options being considered. Low entropy = agent is very confident in one action. Entropy near 0 may indicate the policy has collapsed.
Value Estimate
The critic network's prediction of how likely the agent is to win from the current state. Positive = expects to win, negative = expects to lose. Range is unbounded but typically –1 to +1.
Buy Rate (when affordable)
How often the agent buys a card when it has enough trade to do so. 100% = always buys when it can afford it. Measures card preference independent of economy strength.
Trade / Combat
Trade is currency for buying cards from the trade row. Combat is damage for attacking opponents or destroying bases. Both reset to 0 at end of turn.
Authority (Health)
Each player starts with 50 authority. Reduced by opponent combat. A player at 0 or below loses. Some cards gain authority (healing).
Health Differential
Player's health minus opponent's health at each decision point. Positive = ahead, negative = behind. Shows when games diverge.
Turning Point
The turn in a lost game where the agent's value estimate first goes negative — the moment the critic thinks the game is lost.
Buy Rate Delta
Difference in buy rate between winning and losing games. A large positive delta means the agent buys that card more in wins — it may be a key card to prioritize.
Card Co-occurrence
How often two cards are both purchased in the same winning game. High co-occurrence suggests synergy between cards.
P10 / P90 Range
The 10th and 90th percentile values. The shaded band on economy curves shows where 80% of games fall, filtering out extreme outliers.
Base / Outpost
Bases stay in play across turns (unlike ships). Outposts are bases that must be destroyed before the opponent can attack you or your other bases directly.
